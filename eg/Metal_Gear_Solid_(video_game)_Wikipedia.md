_**Metal Gear Solid**_[d](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid_\(video_game\)#cite_note-8) is a 1998 [action-adventure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Action-adventure_game "Action-adventure game") [stealth game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stealth_game "Stealth game") developed and published by [Konami](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Konami "Konami") for the [PlayStation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_\(console\) "PlayStation (console)"). Directed, produced, and written by [Hideo Kojima](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hideo_Kojima "Hideo Kojima"), the game is the third mainline installment in the [Metal Gear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear "Metal Gear") series, the seventh chronologically, and serves as a sequel to the [MSX2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MSX2 "MSX2") game _[Metal Gear 2: Solid Snake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_2:_Solid_Snake "Metal Gear 2: Solid Snake")_ (1990). The game's story follows [Solid Snake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solid_Snake "Solid Snake"), a former [Special Forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Army_Special_Forces "United States Army Special Forces") operative who is pulled out of retirement by the U.S. government to infiltrate Shadow Moses, a nuclear weapons facility that has been taken over by rogue unit [FOXHOUND](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FOXHOUND "FOXHOUND"), led by [Liquid Snake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_Snake "Liquid Snake"). Solid Snake must liberate hostages and prevent FOXHOUND from using [Metal Gear REX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_REX "Metal Gear REX"), a [mecha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mecha "Mecha") capable of delivering a nuclear warhead anywhere in the world, while confronting his own origins.

The game is played from a [top-down perspective](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Top-down_perspective "Top-down perspective") from where Solid Snake must navigate the environment. Stealth is optional but highly encouraged, with a [cover system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cover_system "Cover system") and radar utilized to avoid sight from guards. Getting detected triggers an "Alert" mode where enemies will attempt to kill the player, and is usually deactivated if they evade guards for long enough. The player obtains various items and weapons through the game, including key cards which are used to unlock different areas of the base, typically obtained from defeating [bosses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boss_\(video_games\) "Boss (video games)").

Conceived by Kojima during the development of _[Policenauts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policenauts "Policenauts")_ (1994), _Metal Gear Solid_ was initially developed for the [3DO Interactive Multiplayer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3DO_Interactive_Multiplayer "3DO Interactive Multiplayer") in 1995 but was soon moved to the PlayStation. With a small team of 20 people, Kojima emphasized extreme detail, utilizing real-life demonstrations of weapons, individually-designed set pieces, and mocking environments using [Lego](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lego "Lego") building blocks. Artist [Yoji Shinkawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoji_Shinkawa "Yoji Shinkawa") designed the majority of characters, including Solid Snake, who was physically based on [Jean-Claude Van Damme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Claude_Van_Damme "Jean-Claude Van Damme") and [Christopher Walken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christopher_Walken "Christopher Walken"). Cinematic [cutscenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cutscenes "Cutscenes") were rendered using the in-[engine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Game_engine "Game engine") graphics, and [voice acting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voice_acting "Voice acting") was used throughout.[^5] _Metal Gear Solid_ was unveiled at the 1996 [Tokyo Game Show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tokyo_Game_Show "Tokyo Game Show") and demonstrated at trade shows including the 1997 [Electronic Entertainment Expo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_Entertainment_Expo "Electronic Entertainment Expo"), to largely positive reception.

Upon release, _Metal Gear Solid_ received unanimous acclaim, with critics lauding the cinematic presentation of the game, narrative, gameplay, and stealth mechanics. Regarded as [one of the greatest and most influential video games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_video_games_considered_the_best "List of video games considered the best") ever made, it is often considered the founding father of the stealth game genre, one of the best PlayStation games, and credited with popularizing cinematic style in video games. Despite low expectations, _Metal Gear Solid_ was also commercially successful, selling more than seven million copies worldwide and establishing the _Metal Gear_ series as a cultural icon. An expanded version, _[Metal Gear Solid: Integral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid:_Integral "Metal Gear Solid: Integral")_, was released in 1999 and 2000 for the PlayStation and [Windows](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows "Windows"), respectively, and both the original and _Integral_ were re-released as part of the [_Metal Gear Solid: Master Collection Vol. 1_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid:_Master_Collection "Metal Gear Solid: Master Collection") in 2023. A direct sequel, _[Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid_2:_Sons_of_Liberty "Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty")_, was released for the [PlayStation 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_2 "PlayStation 2") in 2001. A [remake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_game_remake "Video game remake"), _[Metal Gear Solid: The Twin Snakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid:_The_Twin_Snakes "Metal Gear Solid: The Twin Snakes")_, was released for [GameCube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GameCube "GameCube") in 2004.[^6]

## Gameplay

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/2/25/MGS_screen_psx.jpg/250px-MGS_screen_psx.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:MGS_screen_psx.jpg)

Solid Snake hiding from a guard, behind an [M1 Abrams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M1_Abrams "M1 Abrams") [main battle tank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Main_battle_tank "Main battle tank"). When Snake leans on a corner, the camera shifts to his front for dramatic effect and to enable sight down corridors.

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/MGS_Solton_Radar_Shot.svg/250px-MGS_Solton_Radar_Shot.svg.png)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:MGS_Solton_Radar_Shot.svg)

Representation of the game's 'Soliton Radar' feature. The white dot visual cone represents the [player character](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Player_character "Player character"), while red-dot visual cones represent enemy guards. Green outlines indicate objects or walls the player can hide behind.

The player must navigate the [protagonist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protagonist "Protagonist"), [Solid Snake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solid_Snake "Solid Snake"), through a nuclear weapons facility without being detected by enemies.[^8] When Snake moves into an enemy's [field of vision](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Field_of_vision "Field of vision"), he sets off an "alert mode" that draws enemies.[^9] The player must then hide until "evasion mode" begins; when the counter reaches zero, the game returns to "infiltration mode", where enemies are no longer suspicious. The radar cannot be used in alert or evasion mode. In addition to the stealth gameplay, [set-piece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Set_piece_\(film\) "Set piece (film)") sequences entail firefights between the player and enemies.[^10]

To remain undetected, the player can perform techniques that make use of Snake's abilities and the environment, such as crawling under objects, using boxes as [cover](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cover_System_\(gaming\) "Cover System (gaming)"), ducking or hiding around walls, and making noise to distract enemies. An on-screen [radar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radar "Radar") provides the player with the location of nearby enemies and their field of vision.[^11] Snake can also make use of many items and gadgets, such as [infra-red goggles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermography "Thermography") and a [cardboard box](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardboard_box "Cardboard box") disguise. The emphasis on stealth promotes a less violent form of gameplay, as fights against large groups of enemies will often result in severe damage to Snake.[^12]

Despite the switch to 3D, the game is still played primarily from an overhead perspective similar to the original 2D _Metal Gear_ games.[^13] However, the camera angle will change during certain situations, such as a corner view when Snake flattens himself to a wall next to an open space, or into first-person when crawling under tight spaces or when equipping certain items such as the binoculars or a sniper rifle.[^12] The player can also use the first-person view while remaining idle to look around Snake's surroundings and see what is ahead of him.[^12]

Progress is punctuated by cutscenes and radio conversations, as well as encounters with bosses. To progress, players must discover the weaknesses of each boss and defeat them, which usually yields a [key card](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Key_card "Key card") that is used to unlock different areas of the base and progress.[^12] Play controls and strategies can also be accessed via the Codec, a radio communication device where advice is delivered from Snake's support team; for example, the support team may chastise Snake for not saving his progress often enough, or explain his combat moves in terms of which buttons to press on the gamepad. The Codec is also used to provide [exposition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exposition_\(literary_technique\) "Exposition (literary technique)") on the game's [backstory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backstory "Backstory").[^12]

In addition to the main story, there is also a [VR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtual_reality "Virtual reality") training mode in which the player can test out their sneaking skills in a series of artificially constructed environments.[^12] This mode is divided into three main categories (practice, time attack, and gun shooting), each consisting of ten stages. After completing all 30 stages, a survival mission is unlocked in which the player must sneak their way through ten consecutive stages under a seven-minute limit.[^12]

_Metal Gear Solid_ takes place in an alternate history in which the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War") continued into the 1990s, ending at some point near the end of the 20th century. The game's events take place years after those of _[Metal Gear 2: Solid Snake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_2:_Solid_Snake "Metal Gear 2: Solid Snake")_,[^15] and form the third chapter in an overarching plot concerning the character of Solid Snake.

### Characters

Main article: [List of _Metal Gear_ characters § Introduced in _Metal Gear Solid_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Metal_Gear_characters#Introduced_in_Metal_Gear_Solid "List of Metal Gear characters")

The [protagonist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protagonist "Protagonist") is Solid Snake ([Akio Otsuka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akio_Otsuka "Akio Otsuka")/[David Hayter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Hayter "David Hayter")), a legendary infiltrator and saboteur. During the mission, Snake receives support and advice via codec radio. Colonel [Roy Campbell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_recurring_Metal_Gear_characters#Roy_Campbell "List of recurring Metal Gear characters"), Solid Snake's former [commanding officer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commanding_officer "Commanding officer"), supports Snake with information and tactics. While he initially keeps some secrets from Snake, he gradually reveals them.[^16] He is joined by Naomi Hunter, who gives medical advice; [Nastasha Romanenko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_characters_in_the_Metal_Gear_series#Nastasha_Romanenko "List of characters in the Metal Gear series"), who provides item and weapon tips; [Master Miller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Master_Miller "Master Miller"), a former drill instructor and survival coach; and [Mei Ling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_characters_in_the_Metal_Gear_series#Mei_Ling "List of characters in the Metal Gear series"), who invented the Soliton radar system used in the mission and is also in charge of mission data; the player can call her to save the game.

The main [antagonist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antagonist "Antagonist") of the game is [Liquid Snake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liquid_Snake "Liquid Snake"), leader of a now-terrorist splinter cell of the organization FOXHOUND, and genetic counterpart to Solid Snake.[^10] An elite [special forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_forces "Special forces") unit, FOXHOUND contains experts specializing in different tasks. Members are [Revolver Ocelot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revolver_Ocelot "Revolver Ocelot"), a Western-style gunslinger and expert interrogator whose weapon of choice is the [Colt Single Action Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colt_Single_Action_Army "Colt Single Action Army"); [Sniper Wolf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sniper_Wolf "Sniper Wolf"), a preternatural sniper; [Vulcan Raven](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulcan_Raven "Vulcan Raven"), a hulking Alaskan [shaman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shamanism "Shamanism") armed with an [M61 Vulcan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M61_Vulcan "M61 Vulcan") torn from a downed F-16; [Psycho Mantis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psycho_Mantis "Psycho Mantis"), a psychic profiler and [psychokinesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychokinesis "Psychokinesis") expert; and [Decoy Octopus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_characters_in_the_Metal_Gear_series#Decoy_Octopus "List of characters in the Metal Gear series"), a master of disguise.[^10]

Other characters include [Meryl Silverburgh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meryl_Silverburgh "Meryl Silverburgh"), Colonel Campbell's niece and a rookie soldier stationed in Shadow Moses who did not join the revolt; [Dr. Hal Emmerich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otacon "Otacon"), the lead developer of [Metal Gear REX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_REX "Metal Gear REX"); and Gray Fox, also known as the "[Ninja](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gray_Fox_\(Metal_Gear\) "Gray Fox (Metal Gear)")",[^17] a mysterious cybernetically enhanced agent who is neither an ally nor an enemy of Snake but does oppose FOXHOUND.[^10]

In 2005, renegade genetically enhanced special forces unit FOXHOUND seizes control of a remote island in [Alaska](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alaska "Alaska")'s [Fox Archipelago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fox_Islands_\(Alaska\) "Fox Islands (Alaska)") codenamed "Shadow Moses", which houses a nuclear weapons disposal facility.[^12] FOXHOUND threatens to use the nuclear-capable [mecha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mecha "Mecha") Metal Gear REX, being secretly tested at the facility, against the US government, if they do not receive the remains of [Big Boss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Boss_\(Metal_Gear\) "Big Boss (Metal Gear)") and a ransom of $1 billion within 24 hours.[^18] Snake must liberate hostages and stop the terrorists from launching a nuclear strike.[^19] Solid Snake is forced out of retirement by Colonel Roy Campbell to infiltrate the island and neutralize the threat.[^20]

Snake enters the facility via an air vent and locates the first hostage, [DARPA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DARPA "DARPA") Chief [Donald Anderson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_characters_in_the_Metal_Gear_series#Donald_Anderson "List of characters in the Metal Gear series"). Anderson reveals that Metal Gear REX can be deactivated with a secret detonation override code, but dies of a [heart attack](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myocardial_infarction "Myocardial infarction").[^21] Colonel Campbell's niece [Meryl Silverburgh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meryl_Silverburgh "Meryl Silverburgh"), held hostage in a neighboring cell,[^22] helps Snake escape. Snake locates another hostage, ArmsTech president [Kenneth Baker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_characters_in_the_Metal_Gear_series#Kenneth_Baker "List of characters in the Metal Gear series"),[^23] but is confronted by FOXHOUND member [Revolver Ocelot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revolver_Ocelot "Revolver Ocelot"). Their gunfight is interrupted by a mysterious cyborg ninja who cuts off Ocelot's right hand. Baker briefs Snake on the Metal Gear project and advises him to contact Meryl, whom he gave a PAL card that might prevent the launch, but he too dies of a sudden heart attack.[^24]

Over Codec, Meryl agrees to meet in the warhead disposal area on the condition that Snake contacts Metal Gear's designer, [Dr. Hal "Otacon" Emmerich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Otacon "Otacon"). En route, Snake receives an anonymous codec call calling themselves "[Deepthroat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_Throat_\(Watergate\) "Deep Throat (Watergate)")", warning him of a tank ambush.[^26] Snake fends off the attack from [Vulcan Raven](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulcan_Raven "Vulcan Raven") and proceeds to the rendezvous, where he locates Otacon. The ninja reappears, and Snake realizes it is his former ally [Gray Fox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gray_Fox_\(Metal_Gear\) "Gray Fox (Metal Gear)"), believed dead.[^17] Devastated over learning REX's true intentions, Otacon agrees to aid Snake remotely using special camouflage to procure information and supplies.

Snake meets Meryl and receives the PAL card. As they head for the underground base, Meryl is possessed by psychic [Psycho Mantis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psycho_Mantis "Psycho Mantis") and pulls her gun on Snake. He disarms her and defeats Mantis, who informs Snake that he has "a large place" in her heart. After they reach the underground passageway, [Sniper Wolf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sniper_Wolf "Sniper Wolf") ambushes them, wounds Meryl, and captures Snake. Liquid confirms Snake's suspicion that they are twin brothers.[^27] After being tortured by Ocelot,[^28] Snake is confused to discover Anderson's body in his cell, seemingly dead for days. He escapes with the help of Otacon, makes his way up the communications tower, and fends off a [Hind D](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hind_D "Hind D") helicopter attack from Liquid. As he emerges onto a snowfield, he is confronted again by Sniper Wolf. He kills her, devastating Otacon, who was [infatuated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stockholm_syndrome "Stockholm syndrome") with her.[^29]

Snake continues to REX's hangar and is ambushed again by Raven. After Snake defeats him, Raven tells Snake that the "Anderson" he conversed with was, in fact, FOXHOUND disguise artist Decoy Octopus.[^30] Infiltrating Metal Gear's hangar, Snake overhears Liquid and Ocelot preparing the REX launch sequence and uses the PAL card, but this unexpectedly activates REX.[^31] Liquid reveals that he has been impersonating Snake's advisor Master Miller and that FOXHOUND has used Snake to facilitate REX's launch.[^32] He and Snake are the product of the _Les Enfants Terribles_ project, a 1970s government program to clone Big Boss.[^33] He also reveals to Snake the government's true reason for sending him: Snake is unknowingly carrying a weaponized "FOXDIE" virus that causes cardiac arrest in FOXHOUND members on contact, allowing the government to retrieve REX undamaged.[^34]

As Liquid, in REX, battles Snake, Gray Fox appears. He reveals to Snake that he was Deepthroat, destroys REX's [radome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radome "Radome"), and is crushed to death by REX. Snake destroys REX and defeats Liquid, then escapes with Meryl or Otacon[e](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid_\(video_game\)#cite_note-39) via a tunnel, pursued by Liquid in a [jeep](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeep "Jeep"). After their vehicles crash, Liquid pulls a gun on Snake but dies from FOXDIE.[^35] Colonel Campbell, briefly ousted from command, calls off a nuclear strike to destroy evidence of the operation and has Snake registered as [killed in action](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Killed_in_action "Killed in action") to stop the US government searching for him.[^36] Naomi Hunter, who injected Snake with the FOXDIE virus, tells him that he has an indeterminate amount of time before it kills him. Ocelot calls the [US President](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solidus_Snake "Solidus Snake"); he was a [double agent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Double_agent "Double agent") whose mission was to steal Baker's disk of Metal Gear specifications, and identifies the President as being the secret third clone of [Big Boss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Big_Boss_\(Metal_Gear\) "Big Boss (Metal Gear)").[^37]

## Development

Director [Hideo Kojima](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hideo_Kojima "Hideo Kojima") originally planned his third _Metal Gear_ game in 1994 for the [3DO Interactive Multiplayer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3DO_Interactive_Multiplayer "3DO Interactive Multiplayer").[^38] Kojima was initially planning _Metal Gear Solid_ while _[Policenauts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policenauts "Policenauts")_ (1994), an [adventure game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adventure_game "Adventure game"), was still in development.[^39] Conceptual artwork by [Yoji Shinkawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoji_Shinkawa "Yoji Shinkawa") of the characters [Solid Snake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solid_Snake "Solid Snake"), [Meryl Silverburgh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meryl_Silverburgh "Meryl Silverburgh"), who was also a character in _Policenauts_, and the FOXHOUND team, were included in the _Policenauts: Pilot Disk_ preceding the release of the full 3DO version of _Policenauts_ in 1995.[^40]

The game was titled _Metal Gear Solid_, rather than _Metal Gear 3_, as Kojima felt that the previous [MSX2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MSX "MSX") games that he worked on were not widely known, due to the fact that they were not released in North America and only the first one was released in Europe (an NES version of the first _Metal Gear_ was released in North America, but Kojima had no involvement with it or its sequel _[Snake's Revenge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snake's_Revenge "Snake's Revenge")_).[^41] The word "Solid," derived from the codename of series's protagonist Solid Snake (as well as the title of the second MSX2 game), was chosen not only to represent the fact that it was the third entry of the series, but also the transition from 2D to [3D computer graphics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3D_computer_graphics "3D computer graphics").[^42]

Considering first person games difficult to control, the team opted to give the gameplay a 2D style by having it predominantly played from an overhead angle, while using 3D graphics and the ability to switch to first person on the fly to make it feel as though the game were taking place in a real 3D world.[^43]

Development for _Metal Gear Solid_ began in 1995, but was briefly halted due to the [Great Hanshin earthquake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Hanshin_earthquake "Great Hanshin earthquake") which caused major damage to the development studio.[^44] When development of _Metal Gear Solid_ resumed, it was moved over to the PlayStation platform. Developers aimed for accuracy and realism while making the game enjoyable and tense. In the early stages of development, the [Huntington Beach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huntington_Beach "Huntington Beach") [SWAT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SWAT "SWAT") team educated the creators with a demonstration of vehicles, weapons, and explosives. Weapons expert Motosada Mori was also tapped as a technical adviser in the research, which included visits to [Fort Irwin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fort_Irwin_Military_Reservation "Fort Irwin Military Reservation") and firing sessions at [Stembridge Gun Rentals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stembridge_Gun_Rentals "Stembridge Gun Rentals").[^47] Kojima stated that "if the player isn't tricked into believing that the world is real, then there's no point in making the game." To fulfill this, adjustments were made to every detail, such as individually designed desks.[^49]

The characters and mecha designs were made by artist [Yoji Shinkawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoji_Shinkawa "Yoji Shinkawa") based on Kojima's concepts. When designing props and hardware, he first built plastic models at home, and then drew the final designs from the models.[^50] According to Shinkawa, Solid Snake's physique in this particular installment was based on [Jean-Claude Van Damme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Claude_Van_Damme "Jean-Claude Van Damme"), while his facial appearance was based on [Christopher Walken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christopher_Walken "Christopher Walken").[^51] Konami had decided that the middle-aged appearance of Snake in the previous games did not have good commercial appeal, and opted to redesign him so that he would look younger.[^50] The characters were completed by polygonal artists using brush drawings and clay models by Shinkawa.[^53] According to Kojima, "the ninja's cloaking effect is the result of a bug. Of course, it wasn't totally coincidence since we wanted that effect anyway, but we did get a somewhat unexpected result."[^43] Kojima wanted greater interaction with objects and the environment, such as allowing the player to hide bodies in a storage compartment. Additionally, he wanted "a full orchestra right next to the player"; a system which made modifications such as [tempo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tempo "Tempo") and [texture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Texture_\(music\) "Texture (music)") to the currently playing track, instead of switching to another pre-recorded track. Although these features could not be achieved, they were implemented in _[Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid_2:_Sons_of_Liberty "Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty")_.[^54]

Kojima used [Lego](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lego "Lego") building blocks and [toy figurines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toy_figurine "Toy figurine") to model 3D areas and see what the planned camera views would look like.[^43] The game was developed by a staff of twenty people, a small team for such a major title. Kojima preferred to have a smaller team so that he got to know everyone in the team and what they were working on, and could know if anyone was sick or unhappy.[^39] The team size did not expand to full strength until September 1996; initially, there was only a single programmer working on the game's code.[^56]

Because the developers wanted the game's action to be stylized and movie-like rather than realistic, they opted not to use [motion capture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motion_capture "Motion capture"), instead having an artist with experience in anime design the animations by hand.[^55]

A gameplay demo of _Metal Gear Solid_ was first revealed to the public at the 1996 [Tokyo Game Show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tokyo_Game_Show "Tokyo Game Show") and was later shown at [E3 1997](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E3_1997 "E3 1997") as a short video. The 1997 version had several differences, including a more controllable camera and blue-colored vision cones.[^57] The demo generated significant buzz and positive reviews at the event, for its game design emphasizing stealth and strategy (like earlier _Metal Gear_ games), its presentation, and the unprecedented level of real-time 3D graphical detail for the PlayStation.[^39] The enthusiastic response to the game at E3 took Kojima by surprise, and increased his expectations for the game's performance in the American market.[^43] The game's Japanese release was originally planned for late 1997,[^58] but was delayed to 1998.

It was playable for the first time at the Tokyo Game Show in 1998 and released the same year in Japan[^59] with an extensive promotional campaign.[^47] [Television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Television_advertisement "Television advertisement") and [magazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magazine "Magazine") advertisements, in-store samples, and [demo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Game_demo "Game demo") giveaways contributed to a total of $8 million in promotional costs.[^60]

### Voice acting

Except for [David Hayter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Hayter "David Hayter") (Solid Snake) and [Doug Stone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doug_Stone_\(voice_actor\) "Doug Stone (voice actor)") (Psycho Mantis), the English voice cast was credited with [pseudonyms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudonym "Pseudonym"). Reportedly, this was done because the [Screen Actors Guild](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild "Screen Actors Guild")'s rules at the time were unclear regarding performances for video games. When the actors returned for the 2004 remake _[Metal Gear Solid: The Twin Snakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid:_The_Twin_Snakes "Metal Gear Solid: The Twin Snakes")_, they were credited with their real names.[^61]

## Release

_Metal Gear Solid_ was first released for the PlayStation in Japan on September 3, 1998. The game was available in a standard edition, as well as a limited "Premium Package" edition sold in a large box that also contained a [t-shirt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T-shirt "T-shirt"), a pair of FOXHOUND-themed [dog tags](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dog_tags "Dog tags"), [memory card](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memory_card "Memory card") stickers, an audio CD featuring the soundtracks from the [MSX2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MSX#Evolution "MSX") _Metal Gear_ games (including a few bonus arranged tracks), and a 40-page booklet, _Metal Gear Solid Classified,_ featuring production notes, interviews with the developers, and a glossary of terminology in the game.[^65]

The North American version was released a month later on October 20. Changes and additions were made to this version, such as a choice of three difficulty settings when starting a new game (with a fourth setting that is unlocked after completing the game once), an alternate tuxedo outfit for Snake (which the character wears on every third playthrough on the same save file), and a "demo theater" mode where the player views every cutscene and radio conversations relevant to the main story.[^10] [Jeremy Blaustein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeremy_Blaustein "Jeremy Blaustein"), who previously worked on the English localization of _[Snatcher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snatcher_\(video_game\) "Snatcher (video game)")_ for the [Sega CD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sega_CD "Sega CD"), wrote the English version of the script.[^67] One change in the English script was the addition of Western sources and authors to Mei-Ling's pool of motivational quotes; originally the character only cited Chinese proverbs natively, providing an explanation afterward in Japanese, but this proved challenging to adapt during the translation.[^68] The games detected by Psycho Mantis when he reads the player's memory card were also changed, due to certain games (such as the _[Tokimeki Memorial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tokimeki_Memorial "Tokimeki Memorial")_ series) not being released outside Japan. This resulted in Kojima's cameo (in which he thanks the player for supporting his work via a voiceover) being cut from the Western versions, as save data from two PlayStation games not available outside Japan, _[Snatcher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snatcher_\(video_game\) "Snatcher (video game)")_ and _[Policenauts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Policenauts "Policenauts")_, needed to be present on the player's memory card for this [Easter egg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Easter_egg_\(computing\) "Easter egg (computing)") to appear.[^69]

The game was launched in [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe "Europe") on February 22, 1999, with versions voiced in French, Italian, and German available in addition to English.[^68] A Spanish dubbed version was later released on May 1.[^3] Like in Japan, a limited edition of the game was released, although the contents of the European limited edition differs from the Japanese counterpart. The European Premium Package comes with the game software itself and its soundtrack album CD, along with a t-shirt, dog tags, memory card stickers, a double sided movie-style poster and a set of postcards.[^70]

The Japanese PlayStation version of _Metal Gear Solid_ was reissued twice: once under ["The Best" series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Best_range "The Best range") and later under "PS one Books." Likewise, the American and European versions of _Metal Gear Solid_ were reissued under the "Greatest Hits" and "Platinum" series respectively. For the 20th anniversary of the original _Metal Gear_ in 2007, the original _Metal Gear Solid_ was re-released in Japan as a stand-alone 20th anniversary-themed edition, as well as in the included in the 20th anniversary _Metal Gear Solid Collection_ set,[^71] while in North America it was bundled in the _Metal Gear Solid: Essential Collection_ set.[^72] The original _Metal Gear Solid_ was released on the [PlayStation Store](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_Store "PlayStation Store") for download on the [PlayStation 3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_3 "PlayStation 3") and [PlayStation Portable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_Portable "PlayStation Portable") on March 21, 2008, in Japan[^73] and on June 18, 2009, in North America[^74] and on November 19 of the same year in Europe.[^75]

_Metal Gear Solid_ is one of the twenty PlayStation games included in the [PlayStation Classic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_Classic "PlayStation Classic") released in 2018. The game is included in both the Japanese and western models of the unit in their respective versions.[^76]

### _Integral_

Released on June 25, 1999, for the PlayStation in Japan,[^4] _Metal Gear Solid: Integral_[f](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid_\(video_game\)#cite_note-83) is an expanded edition of the game that features the added content from the American and European versions. It replaces the Japanese voices from the original version with the English dub, offering players a choice between Japanese and English subtitles during cutscenes and CODEC conversations (item descriptions, mission logs, and other text are still in Japanese).[^78] Further additional content to the main game include an alternate "sneaking suit" outfit for Meryl (which she wears when Snake is dressed in the tuxedo), a "Very Easy" difficulty setting where the player starts the mission armed with a suppressor-equipped [MP5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MP5A5 "MP5A5") submachine gun with infinite ammo (substituting the FAMAS rifle in Snake's inventory), an eighth Codec frequency featuring commentary from the development team (unvoiced and in Japanese text only) on every area and boss encounter, hidden music tracks, an alternate game mode where the player controls Snake from a first-person perspective (on Normal difficulty only), an option for alternative patrol routes for enemies, and a downloadable [PocketStation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PocketStation "PocketStation") minigame. The Torture Event was also made easier, reducing the number of rounds to three per session on all five difficulty settings.

The [VR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virtual_reality "Virtual reality") training mode is now stored on a separate third disc, known as the "VR Disc", and has been expanded into 300 missions. These new set of missions are divided into four main categories: Sneaking, Weapons, Advanced, and Special. The first three categories feature standard training exercises that test the player's sneaking, shooting, and combat skills, while the fourth category contains less conventional tests involving murder mysteries, giant genome soldiers, and flying saucers. One particular set of missions has the player controlling the Cyborg Ninja, unlocked by either completing a minigame on the PocketStation and uploading the data to the VR Disc or by achieving the Fox rank on the main game.[^79] Completing all 300 missions will unlock a concept artwork of [Metal Gear RAY](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_\(weapon\)#Metal_Gear_RAY "Metal Gear (weapon)"), a mech that would later appear in _Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty_. Additional content includes preview trailers of _Metal Gear Solid_ from trade events and a photoshoot mode where the player can take photographs of fully expressive polygonal models of Mei Ling and Dr. Naomi after completing the main game.[^68] _[Famitsu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Famitsu "Famitsu")_ magazine rated _Metal Gear Solid: Integral_ a 34 out of 40.[^80]

The VR Disc from _Integral_ was released by itself during the same year in other regions as _Metal Gear Solid: VR Missions_ in North America on September 23 and as _Metal Gear Solid: Special Missions_ in the PAL region on October 29.[^78] While the content of both, _VR Missions_ and _Special Missions_, are virtually identical to the VR Disc, the unlocking requirements for the Ninja missions and the photoshoot mode were changed accordingly, so that save data from the main game was no longer required. The _Special Missions_ version also adds an additional requirement in which the user must also own a copy of the original _Metal Gear Solid_ in PAL format in order to start the game – after booting _Special Missions_ on the console, the player will be asked to switch the disc with the first disc from _Metal Gear Solid_ to load data before asking the player to switch back to the _Special Missions_ disc to proceed through the rest of the game.[^82]

### Windows version

The Windows version of _Metal Gear Solid_ was released in North America on September 22, 2000,[^83] in the United Kingdom on October 20, 2000,[^84] and in other European and Asian territories (excluding Japan) in late 2000.[^78] This version was published by [Microsoft Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xbox_Game_Studios "Xbox Game Studios") and developed by Digital Dialect. It supports the use of a [keyboard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_keyboard "Computer keyboard") or a [USB](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USB "USB") game controller with at least six buttons (with the manual recommending the [Sidewinder Game Pad Pro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft_Sidewinder "Microsoft Sidewinder")). It also supports [Direct3D](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct3D "Direct3D")-capable video cards, allowing for a high resolution of up to 1024x768. The Windows version is labeled _Metal Gear Solid_ on the packaging, but the actual game uses the _Metal Gear Solid: Integral_ logo, although it has some differences as well from the PlayStation version of _Integral_ and lacks some of its content. The most significant change was reducing the number of discs from three to two, which was done by giving each disc two separate [executable files](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/.exe ".exe"), one for the main game (mgsi.exe) and the other for the VR training portion (mgsvr.exe), thus eliminating the need for a stand-alone third disc.

One notable omission was the removal of the cutscene before the Psycho Mantis battle in which he reads the player's memory card and activates the vibration function of the player's controller if a [DualShock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DualShock "DualShock") is being used, as this scene involved the use of PlayStation-specific peripherals. The method for defeating Mantis was also changed from using the second controller to simply using the keyboard (regardless of whether the player was using a game controller or not up to that point). Other omissions include the removal of the eighth Codec frequency (140.07), which featured written commentaries by the developers, Meryl's alternate sneaking suit outfit, and the mission logs when loading a save file. However, the Windows version adds the option to toggle moving and shooting in first-person view mode at any time regardless of difficulty setting, and players can now save their progress at any point without contacting Mei-Ling through the use of quicksaves. On the VR training portion, all 300 missions, as well as the photoshoot mode, are available from the start, although the opening video and the three unlockable preview trailers from the PlayStation version have been removed.[^86]

Scoring 83 on [Metacritic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metacritic "Metacritic")'s aggregate, the game was criticized for "graphic glitches," the aged nature of the port, and being virtually identical to the PlayStation version.[^87]

### _The Twin Snakes_

Main article: [_Metal Gear Solid: The Twin Snakes_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid:_The_Twin_Snakes "Metal Gear Solid: The Twin Snakes")

A remake, _Metal Gear Solid: The Twin Snakes_, was developed by [Silicon Knights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon_Knights "Silicon Knights") under the supervision of KCE Japan and released for the [GameCube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GameCube "GameCube") in North America, Japan, and Europe in March 2004.[^6] Although _Twin Snakes_ was primarily developed at Silicon Knights, its cutscenes were developed in-house at Konami and directed by [Japanese film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinema_of_Japan "Cinema of Japan") director [Ryuhei Kitamura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ryuhei_Kitamura "Ryuhei Kitamura"), reflecting his dynamic signature style, utilizing [bullet time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bullet_time "Bullet time") photography and [choreographed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choreography "Choreography") gunplay extensively.[^88] While the storyline and settings of the game were unchanged (although a select few lines of dialog were re-written more closely resembling the original Japanese version), a variety of gameplay features from _Sons of Liberty_ were added such as the first-person aiming and hanging from bars on walls. Another change in the English voice acting was the reduction of Mei Ling's, Naomi's and Nastasha's accents, as well as the recasting of Gray Fox from [Greg Eagles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greg_Eagles "Greg Eagles"), who still reprises the role of the DARPA chief, to [Rob Paulsen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rob_Paulsen "Rob Paulsen"). The graphics and play mechanics were also updated to match those of _Metal Gear Solid 2_.[^89]

### _Master Collection_ version

The original version of _Metal Gear Solid_ was re-released in October 2023 for [PlayStation 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_4 "PlayStation 4"), [PlayStation 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_5 "PlayStation 5"), [Xbox Series X/S](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xbox_Series_X/S "Xbox Series X/S"), [Nintendo Switch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nintendo_Switch "Nintendo Switch") and [Windows](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Windows "Windows") (via [Steam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steam_\(service\) "Steam (service)")) as part of a series of re-releases titled the _[Metal Gear Solid: Master Collection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid:_Master_Collection "Metal Gear Solid: Master Collection")_. The title is available as stand-alone download with the original _Metal Gear_ and _Metal Gear 2: Solid Snake_ included as bonuses, with all three titles also included as part of a compilation titled _Metal Gear Solid: Master Collection Vol.1_ along with re-releases of _Metal Gear Solid 2_ and _Metal Gear Solid 3_ (both available as separate downloads as well).

In the _Master Collection_, the game runs on an [emulator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_game_console_emulator "Video game console emulator") developed by [M2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M2_\(game_developer\) "M2 (game developer)"), with the option to play any of the seven regional versions (Japanese, North American, European English, German, French, Italian and Spanish) of the game via additional downloads, as well as the _Integral_/_VR Missions_/_Special Missions_ expansions. The _Master Collection_ edition handles the Psycho Mantis' mind reading event by giving the player option to create a virtual memory card with save files from supported games in order to trigger specific lines of dialogue. There's also a new animated sequence when the player reaches the blast furnace which visually depicts the disc-swapping process.[^90]

## Related media

A [Japanese radio drama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_drama_in_Japan "Radio drama in Japan") version of _Metal Gear Solid_, directed by [Shuyo Murata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shuyo_Murata "Shuyo Murata") and written by Motosada Mori, was produced shortly after the release of the original PlayStation game. 12 episodes were aired, from 1998 to 1999 on Konami's _CLUB db_ program. The series was later released on CD as a two-volume series titled _Drama CD Metal Gear Solid_.[^93] Set after the events of the PlayStation game, Snake, Meryl, Campbell and Mei Ling (all portrayed by their original Japanese voice actors) pursue missions in hostile third world nations as FOXHOUND. The new characters introduced include Sgt. Allen Iishiba (voiced by [Toshio Furukawa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toshio_Furukawa "Toshio Furukawa")), a [Delta Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delta_Force "Delta Force") operative who assists Snake and Meryl, Col. Mark Cortez (v.b. [Osamu Saka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Osamu_Saka "Osamu Saka")), an old friend of Campbell who commands the fictional Esteria Army Special Forces, and Capt. Sergei Ivanovich (v.b. [Kazuhiro Nakata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kazuhiro_Nakata "Kazuhiro Nakata")), a former war buddy of Revolver Ocelot from his [SVR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_Intelligence_Service_\(Russia\) "Foreign Intelligence Service (Russia)") days.[^95]

In September 2004, [IDW Publications](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IDW_Publishing "IDW Publishing") began publishing a series of _Metal Gear Solid_ comics,[^97] written by [Kris Oprisko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kris_Oprisko "Kris Oprisko") and illustrated by [Ashley Wood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashley_Wood "Ashley Wood").[^98] The comic was published bimonthly until 2006, lasting 12 issues fully covering the _Metal Gear Solid_ storyline.[^99] The comic was adapted into a [PlayStation Portable](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_Portable "PlayStation Portable") game, _Metal Gear Solid: Digital Graphic Novel_ (_Metal Gear Solid: Bande Dessinée_ in Japan).[^100] It features visual enhancements and two interactive modes designed to give further insight into the publication.[^101] Upon viewing the pages, the player can open a "scanning" interface to search for characters and items in a three-dimensional view.[^101] Discoveries are added to a database which can be traded with other players via [Wi-Fi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wi-Fi "Wi-Fi"). The "mission mode" allows the player to add collected information into a library. This information must be properly connected to complete a mission. _Metal Gear Solid: Digital Graphic Novel_ was released in North America on June 13, 2006, Japan on September 21 and the [PAL region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PAL_region "PAL region") on September 22.[^102] In 2006, the game received [IGN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IGN "IGN")'s award for Best Use of Sound on the PSP.[^103] A [DVD-Video](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DVD-Video "DVD-Video") version is included with its sequel (_Metal Gear Solid 2: Bande Dessinée_), which was released in [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan "Japan") on June 12, 2008. The DVD version features full voice acting.[^104]

A [novelization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Novelization "Novelization") based on the original _Metal Gear Solid_ was written by [Raymond Benson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raymond_Benson "Raymond Benson") and published by Del Rey. The American paperback edition was published on May 27, 2008,[^105] and the British Edition on June 5, 2008.[^106]

A second novelization by Kenji Yano (written under the pen name Hitori Nojima), _Metal Gear Solid Substance I_, was published by [Kadokawa Shoten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kadokawa_Shoten "Kadokawa Shoten") in [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan "Japan") on August 25, 2015.[^107] This novelization is narrated through a text file written by a young man living in Manhattan in 2009 (the present year of the Plant chapter in _[Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid_2:_Sons_of_Liberty "Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty")_). The story also acknowledges certain plot elements from _[Metal Gear Solid V: The Phantom Pain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid_V:_The_Phantom_Pain "Metal Gear Solid V: The Phantom Pain")_ regarding certain characters such as Liquid Snake and Psycho Mantis.

Prior to release, the game's demonstrations at several trade shows between 1996 and 1998 had received a positive response. This had generated significant worldwide interest in the game prior to its release in 1998.[^130]

### Critical reception

_Metal Gear Solid_ received "universal acclaim", according to [review aggregator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Review_aggregator "Review aggregator") website [Metacritic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metacritic "Metacritic").[^108]

_[PlayStation Official Magazine – UK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_Official_Magazine_–_UK "PlayStation Official Magazine – UK")_ review called _Metal Gear Solid_ "the best game ever made. Unputdownable and unforgettable".[^122] The review by _[IGN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IGN "IGN")_ opined _Metal Gear Solid_ came "closer to perfection than any other game in PlayStation's action genre" and called it "beautiful, engrossing, and innovative...in every conceivable category."[^120] _[Computer and Video Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_and_Video_Games "Computer and Video Games")_ compared it to "playing a big budget action blockbuster, only better."[^112] _[Arcade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Future_plc "Future plc")_ magazine praised it for "introducing a brand new genre: the sneak-'em-up" and said it would "herald a tidal wave" of "sneak-'em-ups." They called it a "brilliant, technically stunning, well thought through release that's sure to influence action adventure games for many years."[^124] _[GMR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GMR_\(magazine\) "GMR (magazine)")_ called it a "cinematic classic."[^125]

_[GamePro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GamePro "GamePro")_ called it "this season's top offering [game] and one game no self-respecting gamer should be without," but criticized the [frame rate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frame_rate "Frame rate") that "occasionally stalls the eye-catching graphics."[^116] _[GameSpot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GameSpot "GameSpot")_ was critical of how easy it is for the player to avoid being seen, as well as the game's short length, calling it "more of a work of art than ... an actual game."[^118]

_[Next Generation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Next_Generation_\(magazine\) "Next Generation (magazine)")_ reviewed the PlayStation version of the game, rating it five stars out of five, and stated that "rest assured that this is a game no player should miss and the best reason yet to own a PlayStation."[^121]

_Metal Gear Solid_ received an Excellence Award for Interactive Art at the 1998 [Japan Media Arts Festival](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan_Media_Arts_Festival "Japan Media Arts Festival").[^126] During the [2nd Annual Interactive Achievement Awards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2nd_Annual_Interactive_Achievement_Awards "2nd Annual Interactive Achievement Awards"), the [Academy of Interactive Arts & Sciences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academy_of_Interactive_Arts_&_Sciences "Academy of Interactive Arts & Sciences") nominated _Metal Gear Solid_ for "[Game of the Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D.I.C.E._Award_for_Game_of_the_Year "D.I.C.E. Award for Game of the Year")", "Console Game of the Year", "[Console Action Game of the Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D.I.C.E._Award_for_Action_Game_of_the_Year "D.I.C.E. Award for Action Game of the Year")", and outstanding achievement in "[Interactive Design](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D.I.C.E._Award_for_Outstanding_Achievement_in_Game_Design "D.I.C.E. Award for Outstanding Achievement in Game Design")", "[Software Engineering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D.I.C.E._Award_for_Outstanding_Technical_Achievement "D.I.C.E. Award for Outstanding Technical Achievement")", and "[Character or Story Development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D.I.C.E._Award_for_Outstanding_Achievement_in_Story "D.I.C.E. Award for Outstanding Achievement in Story")".[^131]

In 1999, _[Next Generation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Next_Generation_\(magazine\) "Next Generation (magazine)")_ listed _Metal Gear Solid_ as number 27 on their "Top 50 Games of All Time", commenting that, "_MGS_ is one of the most vibrant efforts in gaming history to bring serious ideas to games."[^132]

### Sales

Prior to its North American release, an estimated 12 million demos for the game were distributed in 1998.[^133] Upon release, the game was a commercial success.[^134] It was the best-selling home console video game in the United States in its first month of release,[^135] became one of the most [rented games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_game_rental "Video game rental") in the territory,[^136] and topped sales charts in the [United Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom "United Kingdom") and [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan "Japan").[^137] [PC Data](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_Data "PC Data"), which tracked sales in the United States, reported that _Metal Gear Solid_ sold 1.06 million copies and earned $51,834,077 (equivalent to $102,388,000 in 2025) in revenue during 1998 alone. This made it the country's fifth-best-selling PlayStation [release of 1998](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1998_in_video_games "1998 in video games"), and the third highest-grossing PlayStation title that year.[^139] In the United Kingdom, it was the third best-selling [video game of 1999](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1999_in_video_games "1999 in video games").[^140] In Germany, it received a Platinum award from the [Verband der Unterhaltungssoftware Deutschland](https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verband%20der%20Unterhaltungssoftware%20Deutschland "de:Verband der Unterhaltungssoftware Deutschland") (VUD) in June 1999 for sales above 200,000 copies within several months,[^141] and it became the year's second best-selling PlayStation game.[^142] In Europe, the game grossed €40,034,122 or $42,668,367 (equivalent to $82,464,333 in 2025) in 1999,[^143] adding up to more than $94,502,444 (equivalent to $186,670,303 in 2025) grossed in the United States and Europe by 1999.

By early 2001, it had sold 6 million units worldwide,[^144] including 1 million units in Japan and approximately 5 million units in the United States and Europe.[^145] It went on to sell more than 6.6 million units worldwide by 2002.[^146] By 2004, the original release had sold 5.51 million and _Integral_ had sold 1.27 million for a combined 6.78 million units worldwide.[^147] As of July 2009, the game had sold over seven million units worldwide.[^148] In the US, 2.81 million units were sold as of 2007.[^150]

Despite its high success even in sales, Kojima, during an interview with [Geoff Keighley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geoff_Keighley "Geoff Keighley") in 2014, revealed that _Metal Gear Solid_ sales expectations were low and said: "Neither I nor anyone else expected _Metal Gear Solid_ to sell at all. [...] I didn't think at all of how to make this game sell well, because I didn't expect it to sell."[^151]

## Legacy

[![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/Hideo_Kojima_shows_Metal_Gear_for_PS_One.jpg/250px-Hideo_Kojima_shows_Metal_Gear_for_PS_One.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Hideo_Kojima_shows_Metal_Gear_for_PS_One.jpg)

Hideo Kojima (with model Yumi Kikuchi) at the 2011 [Tokyo Game Show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tokyo_Game_Show "Tokyo Game Show") holding an original _Metal Gear Solid_ jewel case

_Metal Gear Solid_ is credited with popularizing the [stealth game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stealth_game "Stealth game") genre. The idea of the player being unarmed and having to avoid being seen by enemies rather than fight them has been used in many games since. It is also sometimes acclaimed as being a film as much as a game due to the lengthy [cutscenes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cutscene "Cutscene") and complicated storyline.[^152] _IGN_ called it "the founder of the stealth genre."[^153]

The game is often considered one of the best games for the [PlayStation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PlayStation_\(console\) "PlayStation (console)") and was featured in best video games lists by _[Computer and Video Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_and_Video_Games "Computer and Video Games")_ in 2000,[^154] by _[Electronic Gaming Monthly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_Gaming_Monthly "Electronic Gaming Monthly")_[^155] and _[Game Informer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Game_Informer "Game Informer")_ in 2001,[^156] by _[Retro Gamer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retro_Gamer "Retro Gamer")_ in 2004,[^157] by _[GameFAQs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GameFAQs "GameFAQs")_[^158] and _GamePro_ in 2005,[^159] and by _[Famitsu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Famitsu "Famitsu")_.[^160] _[Hyper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyper_\(magazine\) "Hyper (magazine)")_ magazine in 2001 called it "Probably the single best game on the PlayStation."[^119]

In 2002, _IGN_ ranked it as the best PlayStation game ever, stating that just the demo for the game had "more gameplay [in it] than in most finished titles." _IGN_ also gave it the "Best Ending" and "Best Villain" awards.[^161] In 2005, in placing it 19th on their list of "Top 100 Games", they said that it was "a game that truly felt like a movie."[^153] _[Guinness World Records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinness_World_Records "Guinness World Records")_ awarded _Metal Gear Solid_ with a record for the "Most Innovative Use of a Video Game Controller" for the boss fight with Psycho Mantis in the _[Guinness World Records Gamer's Edition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guinness_World_Records_Gamer's_Edition "Guinness World Records Gamer's Edition")_ 2008 edition.[^163] In 2010, _[PC Magazine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_Magazine "PC Magazine")_ ranked it as seventh in the list of most influential video games of all time, citing its influence on "such stealthy titles as _[Assassin's Creed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assassin's_Creed "Assassin's Creed")_ and _[Splinter Cell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tom_Clancy's_Splinter_Cell "Tom Clancy's Splinter Cell")_."[^164] In 2012, _[Time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_\(magazine\) "Time (magazine)")_ named it one of the 100 greatest video games of all time[^165] and [G4tv](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G4tv "G4tv") ranked it as the 45th top video game of all time.[^166]

According to _[1UP.com](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1UP.com "1UP.com")_, _Metal Gear Solid'_s cinematic style continues to influence modern [action games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Action_game "Action game") such as _[Call of Duty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Call_of_Duty "Call of Duty")_.[^167] _Metal Gear Solid_, along with its sequel, _[Metal Gear Solid 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metal_Gear_Solid_2:_Sons_of_Liberty "Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty")_, was featured in the [Smithsonian American Art Museum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smithsonian_American_Art_Museum "Smithsonian American Art Museum")'s exhibition The Art of Video Games in 2012.[^168] During August 2015, _[Eurogamer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurogamer "Eurogamer")_ reanalyzed the game's technical and overall impact and claimed that _Metal Gear Solid_ had been nothing less than "the first modern video game."[^169] In September 2015, _Metal Gear Solid_ was voted the best original PlayStation game of all time by _PlayStation.Blog's_ users.[^170] In May 2023, _[GQ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GQ "GQ")_ listed _Metal Gear Solid_ as the seventh best video game of all time according to a team of video game journalists across the industry.[^171]
